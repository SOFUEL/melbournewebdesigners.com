WordPress vs Shopify vs Webflow in 2026 — the 10-Minute Decision for Melbourne Businesses

By the MelbourneWebDesigners.com editorial desk · 7 July 2026 · 3 min read

Every week, somewhere in Melbourne, a business pays for the right website on the wrong platform. The build is fine. The design is fine. But two years later they're paying an agency to unpick a decision that took eleven seconds in a kickoff meeting.

The platform question deserves ten minutes. Here they are.

The one-question shortcut

What is your website's job? Not what it might do someday — what it must do in the next 18 months.

  • Sell products online, at any scale → Shopify. Start and end your search there for retail.
  • Publish content, run a business hub, integrate with everything → WordPress.
  • Look exceptional, move fast, minimal maintenance, marketing-led → Webflow.

That heuristic gets it right for about 80% of Melbourne small businesses. The rest of this piece is the other 20%.

Where each platform actually wins

Shopify — the default for e-commerce, full stop

Shopify won Australian e-commerce for a reason: checkout conversion, payments, shipping integrations (AusPost included), fraud handling and an app for every niche. You trade flexibility for reliability — and for a store doing real volume, that's the right trade. Melbourne Shopify builds typically run A$5k–50k+ depending on catalogue complexity and custom design; the platform itself starts around A$56/month plus apps.

Choose something else only if your "store" is really a brochure with three products (WordPress + WooCommerce can carry that), or your product is genuinely bespoke (quotes, configurators, B2B pricing) — in which case you want a Shopify specialist who knows when to bend the platform and when not to fight it.

WordPress — the workhorse for content and complexity

WordPress still powers over 40% of the web because nothing else combines its publishing tools, plugin ecosystem and developer availability. If your growth plan involves serious content — guides, resources, a blog that actually feeds sales — or you need to integrate with CRMs, booking systems, member areas or odd legacy tools, WordPress is the safest long-term bet. Melbourne agencies build excellent custom WordPress sites in the A$3k–10k band.

The honest catch: WordPress is software you own, so it needs maintenance — updates, security, backups. Budget a care plan (typically A$100–300/month locally) or accept the risk. If the thought of "maintaining software" makes you tired, keep reading.

Webflow — the design-forward, low-maintenance option

Webflow produces the kind of sharp, animated, fast marketing sites that win awards — without a plugin stack to babysit. Hosting, security and updates are handled; your marketing team can edit content without breaking layouts. It's become the default for Melbourne startups, agencies and service brands where the website's job is credibility and conversion rather than heavy functionality.

Limits: complex e-commerce, big member systems and deep integrations get awkward or expensive. If that's your roadmap, WordPress (or Shopify for retail) is the sturdier home. For everyone else, a Webflow specialist can ship a premium site in weeks, not months.

The three mistakes that lock businesses into the wrong stack

  1. Choosing the platform your mate's business uses. Their website has a different job. The heuristic above beats anecdote every time.
  2. Letting the agency's comfort zone decide. Plenty of shops sell only what they build. Ask any agency you're vetting: "when would you tell me not to use your main platform?" A good one has a crisp answer. (It's one of the checks in our how to choose a web designer guide.)
  3. Optimising the build price, ignoring the running cost. A cheap WordPress build with no care plan, or a Webflow site that secretly needs three paid add-ons, flips the economics inside a year. Compare 3-year totals: build + platform fees + maintenance + the cost of your team's publishing time.

FAQ

Which platform is cheapest to run long-term?

For a content or brochure site, WordPress on quality Australian hosting is usually cheapest at scale but needs maintenance (updates, security, backups) — budget for a care plan. Webflow costs more monthly (roughly A$25–60+ for site plans) but removes most maintenance. Shopify starts around A$56/month for the core plan plus apps, which is simply the cost of doing serious e-commerce.

Can I move platforms later?

Yes, but it's a project, not a toggle. Content migrates fairly cleanly; design and functionality are rebuilt, not moved. URL redirects must be mapped carefully or you lose your search equity. It's why choosing correctly up front matters — a A$0 platform decision made badly often becomes a A$10k+ replatforming project in year two.

What do Melbourne agencies actually charge on each platform?

In 2026, custom business sites in Melbourne typically land between A$3k–10k on any of the three platforms; e-commerce builds run roughly A$5k–50k+ depending on complexity. Agency hourly rates sit around A$150–200. The platform changes the shape of the cost (build vs ongoing) more than the headline number.

Is Webflow better for SEO than WordPress?

Neither has a meaningful built-in SEO advantage in 2026 — both output clean, fast, indexable sites when built well. What matters is the build quality: page speed, structured data, content architecture and how easily your team can publish. A well-built site on either platform beats a poorly built site on the 'better' one every time.
